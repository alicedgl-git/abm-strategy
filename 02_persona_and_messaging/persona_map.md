## PandaDoc Buying Committee
Based on analysis of 150 leads and sales notes, PandaDoc deals typically involve 3-5 stakeholders:

| Persona | Role Examples | What They Care About | # in Dataset |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Champion / User Buyer | VP of Sales, CRO, Director of Sales | Deal velocity, proposal speed, rep productivity, pipeline visibility | 44 |
| Economic Buyer | CFO, VP of Finance, Director of Finance | ROI, cost reduction, consolidation, risk mitigation | 43 |
| Legal / Compliance | General Counsel, Legal Counsel, Contracts Manager | Compliance, audit trails, version control, redlining, clause libraries | 19 |
| Technical Evaluator | CTO, VP of Operations, Director of Operations | Integrations, security, implementation ease, scalability | 22 |
| Executive Sponsor | COO, CMO | Strategic alignment, competitive advantage, organizational efficiency | 10 |
| Marketing / Other | Marketing Manager, Marketing Specialist | Brand consistency, content management, campaign enablement | 12 |

## Key Insight: Legal is the Blocker
Sales notes repeatedly show Legal/Compliance as the primary blocker in PandaDoc deals:
- *'Legal team is the primary blocker — they want custom clause libraries and redlining'*
- *'General Counsel needs a separate demo'*
- *'VP of Operations (General Counsel) needs a separate demo'*

**Implication:** Every ABM play should include Legal-specific content and a path to a compliance-focused demo.
